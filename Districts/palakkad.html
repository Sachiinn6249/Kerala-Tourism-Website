Palakkad

Palakkad is one of the fourteen districts of Kerala and has no coastal line. The
district opens the state to the rest of the country through the Palakkad Gap
with a width of 32 to 40 Kms. Its geographical position, historical background,
educational status, tourism hot-spots and above all, the development
activities that are carried out, are wide and varied. The district is one of the
main granaries of Kerala and its economy is primarily agricultural. The district
is also the land of Palmyrahs.

About

Palakkad, or Palghat, is a city in Kerala, a state in southwestern India. The 18th-century Palakkad Fort has sturdy battlements, a moat and a Hanuman temple on its grounds. North, on the Kalpathy River, the 15th-century Viswanatha Swamy Temple is the main venue of the famous Ratholsavam chariot festival.

Northeast, near Malampuzha Dam, the town of Malampuzha has a rock garden created from recycled materials.
